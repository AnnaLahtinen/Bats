Plecotus auritus

    Brown long-eared bat

  • Weight: 6-12g
  • Length: 4.5-4.8cm
  • Wingspan: 20-30cm
  • Lifespan: 4-30 years
  • Can be found in Europe and Asia.
  • Light grey-brown fur and a pale underside.
  • Distinguishable ears 3.3-3.9cm
  • Social creatures, mostly breed and roost in groups.
  • Relatively slow flyers compared to other species.
  • Detect prey using sight and sound, not echolocation. Unlike most bats, can detect prey using only sound due to sensitive hearing.
  • Usually hibernate between November and April.

pipistrellus pipistrellus

    Common pipistrelle bat

  • Weight: 3.5-8.5g
  • Length: 3.5-5cm
  • Wingspan: 18-25cm
  • Lifespan: 4-16 years
  • Can be found in Europe, Asia and Africa.
  • Brown fur, black wings and face.
  • Very small.
  • Live in large colonies.
  • Use echolocation to hunt prey, frequencies lie between 45 kHz and 76 kHz
  • Roost during the day.
  • Common in woodland and farmland.
  • Start hibernating around mid-October and hibernate till spring.

Tadarida brasiliensis

    Mexican free-tailed bat

  • Weight: 7-12g
  • Length: 9cm
  • Wingspan: 28-36cm
  • Lifespan: 8-18 years
  • Can be found in North America and South America
  • Dark brown or grey fur, large forward pointing ears and wrinkled lips
  • Long tail which unlike in most bats extends freely beyond the tail membrane
  • Live in large colonies
  • Use echolocation for detecting prey and navigating.
  • They are migratory bats, so they do not hibernate. During winter most populations move to Mexico and Central America
  • Claimed to have the fastest horizontal speed of any animal, reaching top ground speeds over 162 km/h

Eptesicus fuscus

    Big brown bat

  • Weight: 15-26g
  • Length: 9-14cm
  • Wingspan: 32.5-35cm
  • Lifespan: 6.5-20 years
  • Can be found in North America, South America and Africa
  • Dark brown/copper to blond fur. The muzzle is distinctively round and dark
  • Ears quite short with rounded tips
  • Use echolocation to hunt prey and navigate
  • Hibernate around November, often by themselves or in small groups and come out of hibernation in the spring
  • They are often solitary, so they do not live in colonies. Males may form small all-male colonies

Myotis daubentonii

    Daubenton's bat

  • Weight: 5-15g
  • Length: 4.5-5.5cm
  • Wingspan: 24-27.5cm
  • Lifespan: 3-22 years
  • Can be found in Europe and Asia
  • Fluffy fur that is brownish-grey on the back and silvery-grey on the underside
  • They have black wings and reddish-pink faces
  • Use echolocation to hunt prey and navigate in frequencies between 32 kHz and 85 kHz
  • Hunts prey just above water, can also use it's tail and feet to scoop up insects from the water's surface
  • Usually form colonies during the summer, but tend to hibernate alone. They start hibernating around October and end hibernation at the onset of spring

Eptesicus serotinus

    Serotine bat

  • Weight: 15-35g
  • Length: 6-8cm
  • Wingspan: 37cm
  • Lifespan: up to 19 years (Average 5 years)
  • Can be found in Europe and Asia
  • Dark brown fur and a dark brown or black face, wings and ears. Yellowish-brown belly
  • Ears are large and pointed
  • Usually solitary, but females form maternity colonies around May. Males may form small colonies separate from females
  • Use echolocation to hunt prey, frequencies change from 15 kHz to 65 kHz
  • Usually hibernate alone or in small groups during winter. Are quite tolerant to cold, so a few of them have been spotted flying around in winter
